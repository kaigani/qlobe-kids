# Code of Conduct

QLOBE Kids is made for five-year-olds, and we hold grown-ups to at least the
same standard of kindness we design into the games.

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in
this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age,
body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Being kind and patient, especially with first-time contributors and
  parents who are new to code
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the kids who play these games
- Showing empathy toward other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sexualized language or imagery of any kind — this is a children's project
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  setting that includes children's families

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening an issue on
[github.com/kaigani/qlobe-kids](https://github.com/kaigani/qlobe-kids/issues)
or contacting the maintainer directly. All complaints will be reviewed and
investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject
contributions and may temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for
behaviors they deem inappropriate.

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org), version 2.1.
