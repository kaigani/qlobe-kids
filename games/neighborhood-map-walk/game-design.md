### Mini-GDD: Neighborhood Map Walk (games/neighborhood-map-walk/)

**Category:** culture-geography · **Engine:** coach-timer · **splashEmoji:** 🗺️
**Premise:** the child becomes a mapmaker: draw home in the middle of a big
paper, then the tablet coaches a real walk (or window study) adding the
things they know — the big tree, the shop, the postbox — each landmark drawn
onto THEIR map when they return.
**Learning:** spatial thinking, landmark memory, map symbols, my-place-in-the-
world geography.
**Modes:**
1. `map` "Make Your Map" (type steps): 1 draw your home in the middle of the
   paper (emoji:🏠) → 2 which way does your door face? draw the street
   (emoji:🛣️) → 3 add the thing you see FIRST when you step out, 60s
   (emoji:🌳) → 4 add two more favorite landmarks, 90s (emoji:🏪) → 5 draw
   the route to your favorite place as a dotted line (emoji:➿).
2. `walk` "Walk and Spot" (type steps): a grown-up walk with spotting
   missions — something red (emoji:🔴), a number on a door (emoji:🔢), a
   thing taller than a grown-up (emoji:📏), something that grows (emoji:🌱);
   add each to the map at home, 3min (emoji:🖍️).
**Voice tone:** expedition leader; the child's map is THE map, no wrong
streets.
**Assets needed:** art — crayon-style map elements (house, tree, shop, path);
voice — all config lines.
