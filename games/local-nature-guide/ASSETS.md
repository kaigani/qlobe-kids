# Local Nature Guide Assets

All current art is emoji placeholder art rendered by the `coach-timer` engine on soft rounded cards. No image files, generated art, downloaded art, recorded clips, or network assets are used. The Field Journal background at `./assets/bg.jpg` is a declared production-art slot and is not included in this stub.

## Assets needed

Art:

- Pinecone or seed treasure.
- Small collection of special rocks.
- Varied fallen leaves.
- Nature-museum display cloth.
- Family nature guide badge.

Voice lines:

- Welcome, Nature Guide! Every tiny treasure has a story to share.
- Wonderful guide work. You noticed something special!
- Tour complete! You are your family's nature guide!
- Guide training complete! Your nature treasures are ready for a tour!
- Find one fallen pinecone or seed thing. Collect only what nature has finished using.
- Find one special rock. What makes it museum-grade to you?
- Rock found! Carry your tiny treasure gently.
- Find one interesting fallen leaf. Leave growing leaves on their plants.
- Study each treasure like a nature guide. Feel it, smell it safely, and look very close.
- Excellent observing! You know your treasures better now.
- Practice a guide sentence for each treasure. Say what it is, where it comes from, and what someone can notice.
- Your guide voice is ready for visitors!
- Your visitor is learning from a real nature guide!
- Magnificent tour! It is time for your guide badge ceremony!
- Arrange your nature museum on a cloth. Give every treasure a special place.
- Invite your visitor to the museum. Welcome them like a warm park ranger!
- Present each treasure with your guide sentence. For example: This is a pinecone. It comes from a pine tree. Feel how bumpy!
- What a tour! Every treasure got its museum moment.
- Ask your visitor for one question. It is okay to say, I do not know yet. Let us wonder together!
- Guide badge ceremony! Ask your visitor to place an imaginary badge on your shirt and cheer, Nature Guide!
