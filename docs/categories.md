# QLOBE Kids — Categories

QLOBE Kids organizes games into 10 broad, expandable learning areas. Each area can hold many game families, and each family can hold many mini-games.

**Categories are metadata, not folders.** They live in `games.json` (the central registry) as an ordered list, and each game names its category by id. Games themselves live flat in `games/<id>/`. Do not create category folders — a game moves between categories by changing one field.

The design philosophy for all categories is shared: see [philosophy.md](philosophy.md).

The 10 categories, in registry order:

## 1. Reading & Phonics — `reading-phonics`
Letter sounds, blending, rhyme, and early word-building — hearing and seeing language before reading it fluently.
- *Example families:* Sound Sprouts (onset-rime word building, the reference game); a rhyme-matching family; a beginning-sounds "which one starts with…" family.

## 2. Writing & Fine Motor — `writing-fine-motor`
Pre-writing control and mark-making: tracing shapes and letters, guiding lines, and building the hand strength behind writing.
- *Example families:* a letter-tracing family; a dot-to-dot / line-following family; a "trace the path" maze family.

## 3. Math & Number Sense — `math-number-sense`
Counting, quantity, comparison, and early number relationships — grounded in objects a child can move and count.
- *Example families:* a counting-and-tapping family; a sorting-and-comparing (more/fewer) family; a pattern-making family.

## 4. Practical Life & Independence — `practical-life`
Everyday self-care and sequencing: getting dressed, tidying, pouring, hand-washing, and doing steps in order.
- *Example families:* a "get ready" dress/pack-the-bag sequencing family; a sorting-and-tidying family; a "what comes next" daily-routine family.

## 5. Sensorial & Early Science — `sensorial-science`
Noticing and classifying the world: colors, sizes, textures, sounds, sink/float, living/non-living, and simple cause and effect.
- *Example families:* a sorting-by-attribute (size/color/texture) family; a "listen and match the sound" family; a living-vs-not classification family.

## 6. Oral Language & Storytelling — `oral-storytelling`
Talking, listening, and making meaning: retelling, sequencing a story, silly-sentence building, and emotion voice play.
- *Example families:* a picture-sequencing "tell the story" family; a silly-sentence builder; a puppet-style retelling / emotion-voice family.

## 7. Culture, Geography & History — `culture-geography`
A first look outward: places, homes, food, animals, flags, weather, and how families live around the world.
- *Example families:* a "where does this belong" places-and-homes family; a world-animals / habitats family; a flags-and-greetings matching family.

## 8. Art & Music — `art-music`
Creative expression: color mixing, simple drawing and stamping, rhythm tapping, and playful instrument sounds.
- *Example families:* a color-mixing / paint-and-stamp family; a rhythm-and-beat tapping family; a "play the melody" instrument family.

## 9. Movement & Outdoor Learning — `movement-outdoor`
Body-aware play that gets a child moving: clap, hop, stretch, freeze, and act-it-out prompts anchored to a tablet game.
- *Example families:* a "freeze dance" / follow-the-move family; an animal-movement mimic family; a scavenger "go find something…" family.

## 10. Social-Emotional & Self-Regulation — `social-emotional`
Feelings, calm, and kindness: naming emotions, breathing and calming games, taking turns, and gentle empathy practice.
- *Example families:* a name-the-feeling matching family; a breathing / calm-down family; a "what would help?" kindness-choice family.
